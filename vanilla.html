Step-by-Step Visual Guide

Step 1: Whip the eggs and sugar until thick and glossy.

Step 2: Whisk together flour, baking powder, and salt.

Step 3: Melt butter and warm milk together.

Step 4: Fill cupcake liners with the prepared batter.

Step 5: Bake for 22 minutes until golden.

Step 6: Frost and enjoy your beautiful cupcakes!

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 eggs (Do not forget! Room Temperature, not fridge cold)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 100g butter
  • ½ cup milk, full fat

    • For Frosting

    • 50g butter
    • 100ml whipping cream
    • 1 coffee spoon salt
    • 200g powdered sugar
    • 1 tbsp vanilla extract

Preparation

  1. Beat eggs until fluffy—For better results, use a handheld beater instead.
  2. Whip the eggs and sugar—Beat the eggs with sugar for a whole 7 minutes until thick and glossy.
  3. Whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt (dry ingredients).
  4. Gradually add the flour mixture into the egg mixture in 3 lots, mixing for just 5 seconds on Speed 1 in between.
  5. Melt butter and heat milk—Use a microwave or stove to heat milk with the butter until it melts.
  6. Slowly pour the mixture back into whipped eggs over 15 seconds while whipping on the lowest speed. Scrape down the sides of the bowl, then mix for ten seconds.
  7. Fill cupcake liners with batter.
  8. Bake for 22 minutes until golden and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  9. Remove after 2 minutes—Rest for just 2 minutes to let them cool slightly, then get them out of the muffin tin ASAP, otherwise, they will continue cooking and may overcook.

For Topping

  1. Cream the softened butter and salt in a stand mixer for about a minute using a paddle attachment. You can also make this frosting with an electric hand mixer and a big bowl.
  2. Add powdered sugar to the butter in batches. Sift the powdered sugar to avoid clumps that may cause clogs if you are using piping tips.
  3. After the first batch of powdered sugar is added, scrape down the bowl and sift in more powdered sugar. Repeat this process.
  4. Add a tablespoon of cream or milk and mix in. You can add more later if desired.
  5. Once incorporated, with the mixer running on low, add the vanilla extract. Add more cream or milk as needed to adjust the consistency.
  6. Once finished, you can use the frosting immediately or chill it and bring it to room temperature before using. Whip it again if necessary.
